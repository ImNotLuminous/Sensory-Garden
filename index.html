About Our Sensory Garden

The Sensory Garden Project started as a Tech Healthcare class assignment and turned into something a lot bigger. The idea was simple: pick a plant, research how it connects to mental health and sensory therapy, and bring it to life in a real garden space that students could actually use.

Every plant here was chosen and researched by a student in our class. Each person dug into their plant's history, its therapeutic properties, and why it belongs in a space like this. Some plants are calming, some are interactive, some are just really interesting to look at or touch. The variety is kind of the point.

The website was built so that anyone walking through the garden can scan a QR code at a plant and pull up the student's full research and Canva presentation right on their phone. No app, no sign in, just scan and read.

Overall this project was a way for our class to think about healthcare a bit differently. Not just doctors and hospitals, but the smaller things too, like having a quiet place to sit outside, or noticing something interesting growing in the ground.
